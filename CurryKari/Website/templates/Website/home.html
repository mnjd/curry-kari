Indian Food Understanding Indian food philosophy

There is not one Indian cuisine but there are Indian cuisines. The same thing can be said for Indian culture, although, there is an unity in India. Indian cuisines have a philosophy, which identify Indian foods. Indian cuisines tend to blend various and numerous ingredients into a perfect marriage. Garam masala power is for instance a mix of 7 to 20 spices.

The ultimate goal resides in making a perfect dish in which each ingredient gives its best to the others.



Transformation of products is key here. The raw products whether meat, sea food or vegetables cannot be preserved in their initial form. A lamb leg curry will be looking the same as lamb breast one but the taste will be so different not only because of the meat parts but also ingredients used. This philosophy differs greatly from other cuisines like French, Japanese, Chinese...
